Ha Long Bay

Vietnam’s Ha Long Bay is famous for its pillar-like limestone formations formed over thousands of years of karsting activity. Among the bay’s limestone seascape sits the island of Cát Bà, home to a large national park. Flowing into the bay are the instrumental rivers of Sông Hồng, Sông Chảy, and Thai Binh.
